About the Society

We make theatre
in the dark.

Founded in Los Angeles, Society of the Cellar is a collective of theatre makers dedicated to dismantling conventions. We create work that exists in the liminal space between performance and provocation.

Our productions excavate forgotten histories, challenge dominant narratives, and reimagine theatrical form. We believe theatre is a radical act, a space where audiences do not simply watch, but participate in collective transformation.

We are drawn to what happens when faith is institutionalized, when power masquerades as righteousness, and when private belief becomes violently public law.

"Theatre should disturb the comfortable and comfort the disturbed."

Radical Aesthetic

Work that uses comedy and satire as weapons. Accessible on the surface, subversive underneath, and aimed at targets most theatre won't touch.

Historical Subversion

We excavate the past not to preserve it but to interrogate it, using anachronism, dark comedy, and the surreal logic of the present.

Collective Practice

Every production is built in collaboration. We welcome directors, performers, writers, and the genuinely difficult question.

Our Collaborators

Jan-David Soutar

Founding Director and Writer

Playwright, screenwriter, and director. Writer and director of Oh Constantine! His work draws on history, dark comedy, and the surreal logic of modern America.

Athena Rethis

Producer

Award Winning Director of A.D. 33 & Producer of Oh Constantine! at the Hollywood Fringe Festival 2025. Athena is a core architect of the Society's productions from development through opening night.

Megan Rippey

Executive Producer

Writer/Director of her solo show "Confessions of a Pregnant Stripper" at Hollywood Fringe 2026, Megan is also the Executive Producer and founding force behind the Society of the Cellar. Megan brings strategic vision, passion and deep creative partnership to every project the collective undertakes.
